Creators

Sarah Mack & Lauren Dortch-Crozier were finished waiting around for permission to tell stories.


They vowed to take their artistic paths into their own hands, to work with as many powerful women they could, and to make people laugh through their own personal stories. They're also okay with the word vagina.

Sarah Mack

has 7 younger brothers and sisters and she was not in a cult. She is, however, open to the possibility of cult leadership in the future . She has been seen on stage and screen across the country for over 20 years. in 2015 Sarah and fellow actress, Lauren Dortch- Crozier created 'I'd Ride That Pony Productions', a production company committed to helping female artists tell their stories. The two of them wrote and produced their own dark comedy series, 'It's Fine' that is set to premier September 2016.

Lauren Dortch-Crozier

is a comedic actress and coffee addict who calls Brooklyn home. She Sarah founded the female driven production company I'd Ride That Pony in 2015 with the goal of creating work and supporting women in the entertainment industry. Lauren's credits include Off and Off-Off Broadway theatre in New York, rolling world premieres throughout the southeast, as well as various national commercial, voiceover and film credits. She’s been featured on the cover of American Theatre Magazine, and called “Nothing short of astonishing” by Creative Loafing.
